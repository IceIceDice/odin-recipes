Blinni

Description

Russian Blini are thin and delicate pancakes that hold a sacred place in Slavic culinary tradition. Unlike thick American pancakes or even French crêpes, authentic blini are incredibly soft, buttery, and often feature a beautiful, lace-like pattern of tiny holes. Historically celebrated during Maslenitsa - the sun-welcoming spring festival - they are a symbol of warmth and hospitality. They are incredibly versatile and can be wrapped around savory fillings like smoked salmon and caviar, or sweet toppings like sour cream, jam, and honey.

Ingridients

  • 500ml milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 200g white flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 50g butter

How to cook:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk the eggs, sugar, and salt together until slightly frothy. Pour in about half of the warmed milk and mix. Sift in the flour gradually, whisking constantly to ensure there are no lumps. Once the batter is smooth and thick, pour in the remaining milk and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Whisk until completely combined; the batter should be very thin, resembling the consistency of heavy cream.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or a traditional cast-iron crêpe pan over medium-high heat. Lightly brush the surface with a tiny bit of vegetable oil using a paper towel or silicone brush. The pan must be very hot before the first blini goes in.
  3. Lift the hot pan off the heat slightly. Ladle a small amount of batter (about 1/4 cup) into the center of the pan while quickly tilting and rotating it in a circular motion to spread the batter into a thin, even circle. Work fast, as the batter cooks almost instantly!
  4. Place the pan back on the heat. Cook for about 1 to 1.5 minutes. You will see the edges start to turn golden brown and lift slightly, and tiny bubbles will form across the surface. Carefully slide a thin spatula underneath, flip the blini over, and cook the other side for just 30 seconds.
  5. Transfer the cooked blini to a large plate. Immediately brush the top of the hot blini with a little bit of melted butter. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, stacking the finished blini directly on top of each other to keep them soft and warm.
  6. Serve them warm! Fold them into triangles or roll them up. Top with a generous dollop of sour cream, your favorite berry jam.
